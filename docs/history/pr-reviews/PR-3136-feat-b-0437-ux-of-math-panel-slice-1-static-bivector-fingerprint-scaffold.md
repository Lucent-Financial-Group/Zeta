  <pre>
  The `score` field is rounded to two decimals here, and then `uxmRenderScoreBoard` does `Math.round(clause.score * 100)` again, and `renderUxOfMathTab` computes the mean from these already-rounded values. This double-rounding causes the displayed percentages and mean to drift from the true v₁·v₂ product (and contradicts the "score = adherence × evidence = 0.85 × 0.81 = 0.69" claim, where the unrounded value is 0.6885). Store the raw product on the clause and round only at display time to keep the displayed scores and the computed mean consistent.

  </pre>

  Providing both `aria-label` and child fallback text on the same `&lt;canvas&gt;` causes some assistive technologies to ignore the fallback content (the `aria-label` wins). The fallback paragraph inside the canvas contains the most descriptive information (numeric values, what the shaded rectangle represents) and won't be exposed to screen readers in that case.
