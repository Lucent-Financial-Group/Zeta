## 1 — Autopoiesis, self-organization, and origin-of-life

**Humberto Maturana + Francisco Varela** — *Autopoiesis and Cognition* (1980); *The Tree of Knowledge* (1987).
Autopoiesis: a system that produces and maintains its own components. The self-reference
substrate: biological cognition as structurally-coupled, not representational. B-0045
names this as a key candidate for factory-operator-algebra resonance.

**Stuart Kauffman** — *The Origins of Order* (1993); *At Home in the Universe* (1995).
Autocatalytic sets as the origin-of-life candidate; order-for-free as a complement
to natural selection. Complex adaptive systems at the boundary between order and chaos.
B-0045 names autocatalytic sets directly as a Stage-1 candidate.

**Manfred Eigen** — *Hypercycle: A Principle of Natural Self-Organization* (1979, with Schuster).
Hypercycles as self-replicating molecular networks. Precursor to modern origin-of-life theory.
Connects to Kauffman's autocatalytic sets.

**Ilya Prigogine** — *Order Out of Chaos* (1984, with Stengers).
Dissipative structures: far-from-equilibrium thermodynamics produces self-organized order.
The physics grounding for biological complexity. Nobel Prize 1977.

**Erwin Schrödinger** — *What Is Life?* (1944).
The founding document bridging physics and biology: negentropy, aperiodic crystal as the
genetic material, quantum mechanics in biology. Directly inspired Watson and Crick.

## 2 — Endosymbiosis and major evolutionary transitions

**Lynn Margulis** — *Symbiosis in Cell Evolution* (1981); *Microcosmos* (1987, with Dorion Sagan).
Endosymbiotic theory: mitochondria and chloroplasts descended from free-living prokaryotes
absorbed by host cells. The mitochondrion retains its own genome — harmonious division
inside unification (structural yin-yang note, flagged for Stage 2). Challenged neo-Darwinian
orthodoxy; confirmed by molecular evidence.

**John Maynard Smith + Eörs Szathmáry** — *The Major Transitions in Evolution* (1995).
Eight transitions in which independently replicating units merged into larger cooperative
wholes: genes → chromosomes → eukaryotes → multicellularity → societies. Each transition
involves a shift in how information is stored and transmitted. A structural account of
biological unification moves.

**Lynn Cavelier** — lineage-tracking as retraction-log. [Note: B-0045 names this candidate;

*The Origin of Eukaryote and Archaebacterial Cells* (1987) and subsequent kingdom-level
phylogeny revisions — known for systematic retraction and revision of eukaryote
classification.] Stage 2 to confirm exact source.

## 3 — Development and morphogenesis

**Lewis Wolpert** — *The Triumph of the Embryo* (1991); *Principles of Development* (2002, with others).
Positional information: cells acquire identity by reading their position in the embryo.
Fate-specification as a coordinate-assignment problem. B-0045 names this as
"operator-assignment" resonance candidate.

**Conrad Hal Waddington** — *The Strategy of the Genes* (1957).
Epigenetic landscape: developmental canalization visualized as valleys in a landscape.
Canalization = robustness of developmental trajectories to perturbation. Named
"epigenetics" before the molecular mechanism was known.

**Alan Turing** — *The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis* (1952).
Reaction-diffusion systems as the mechanism for biological pattern formation.
Turing patterns explain zebra stripes, finger formation, and skin pigmentation.
One of the earliest formal models of biological self-organization. Cross-field:
Turing's morphogenesis paper connects to his CS foundations.

## 4 — Molecular biology and genetics

**Gregor Mendel** — *Versuche über Pflanzenhybriden* (1866).
Discrete inheritance units (genes before the word existed). The founding combinatorial
model of heredity. Rediscovered 1900.

**James Watson + Francis Crick** — *Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids* (1953).
Double helix structure of DNA. The information-substrate of heredity made physical.
Schrödinger's aperiodic crystal identified.

**François Jacob + Jacques Monod** — *Genetic Regulatory Mechanisms in the Synthesis
of Proteins* (1961, J. Mol. Biol.).
Lac operon: the first molecular gate — a gene regulatory network that switches protein
production on/off in response to substrate availability. Boolean logic in biology.

**Barbara McClintock** — *The Origin and Behavior of Mutable Loci in Maize* (1950).
Transposable elements (jumping genes): genomic sequences that move within and between
genomes. The genome is not static; it has internal rewriting operators. Nobel Prize 1983.

**Francis Crick** — *Central Dogma of Molecular Biology* (1958/1970).
Information flows DNA → RNA → protein; not reverse. The canonical flow rule.
Reverse transcription (retroviruses) is a known exception; not a refutation of the
general direction.

## 5 — Evolution

**Charles Darwin** — *On the Origin of Species* (1859).
Natural selection as the mechanism of evolutionary change. The founding text.

**Ronald A. Fisher** — *The Genetical Theory of Natural Selection* (1930).
Mathematical synthesis of Mendelian genetics and Darwinian selection. The Modern
Synthesis foundation on the quantitative genetics side.

**Theodosius Dobzhansky** — *Genetics and the Origin of Species* (1937).
The Modern Synthesis: reconciling population genetics with paleontology and systematics.
Famous framing: "Nothing in biology makes sense except in the light of evolution."

**Stephen Jay Gould + Richard Lewontin** — *The Spandrels of San Marco and the
Panglossian Paradigm* (1979, Proc. R. Soc. Lond.).
Spandrels: architectural by-products that acquire function. A critique of strict
adaptationism; argues that not every feature is an adaptation. The yin-yang pair
for adaptationism: constraint + selection, not selection alone.

**Stephen Jay Gould + Niles Eldredge** — *Punctuated Equilibria* (1972).
Evolution proceeds in bursts (punctuation) separated by long stasis, not gradual
continuous change. Tempo as a variable, not assumed constant.

**William D. Hamilton** — *The Genetical Evolution of Social Behaviour* (1964, J. Theor. Biol.).
Inclusive fitness: altruism evolves when Hamilton's rule (rb > c) is satisfied.
Kin selection as the mathematical mechanism for cooperative behavior.

## 6 — Systems biology

**Denis Noble** — *The Music of Life* (2006); *Dance to the Tune of Life* (2017).
Systems biology reframed: genes do not program organisms; organisms use genes.
The organicist counter to gene-centrism. Noble also rehabilitated Lamarck (epigenetic
inheritance of acquired characters). B-0045 names Noble directly.

**Uri Alon** — *An Introduction to Systems Biology* (2006).
Network motifs: the small recurring circuit patterns (feedback loops, coherent
feedforward loops) that recur across biological networks from bacteria to neurons.
Engineering-style analysis of biological circuits.

**Hiroaki Kitano** — *Systems Biology: A Brief Overview* (2002, Science).
Founding framing paper for systems biology as a discipline. Robustness as a central
organizing principle of biological systems.

## 7 — Thermodynamics and life

**Jeremy England** — *Statistical Physics of Self-Replication* (2013, J. Chem. Phys.).
Living systems dissipate energy at near-optimal rates given their structure.
Darwinian adaptation may be a special case of thermodynamic optimization. Contested;
included as a probe.

**Nick Lane** — *Power, Sex, Suicide: Mitochondria and the Meaning of Life* (2005);
*The Vital Question* (2015).
Bioenergetics as the key to understanding the complexity of eukaryotic life.
Why proton gradients across membranes are the universal energy currency.

**Alfred Lotka** — *Elements of Physical Biology* (1925).
Lotka-Volterra predator-prey equations. The first population-dynamics model with

**E.O. Wilson** — *Sociobiology* (1975); *The Diversity of Life* (1992).
Sociobiology: evolutionary mechanisms for social behavior. Biodiversity measurement
and conservation. Both contentious and influential.

## 9 — Lineage, phylogeny, and classification

**Carl Woese + George Fox** — *Phylogenetic Structure of the Prokaryotic Domain* (1977, PNAS).
16S rRNA as the universal phylogenetic clock. Archaea as a distinct domain of life
separate from bacteria. The three-domain tree of life.

**Thomas Cavalier-Smith** — *A Revised Six-Kingdom System of Life* (1998, Biol. Rev.);
multiple papers revising eukaryote kingdom structure.
Systematic classification and reclassification of eukaryotes. Lineage-tracking as
a retraction-log: each revision is a dated correction of prior classification.

## 10 — Cognition, embodiment, and biology-mind interface

**Francisco Varela + Evan Thompson + Eleanor Rosch** — *The Embodied Mind* (1991).
Enactivism: cognition is not computation over representations but active engagement
of organism with environment. Extends Maturana+Varela autopoiesis to cognitive science.

**Antonio Damasio** — *Descartes' Error* (1994); *The Feeling of What Happens* (1999).
Somatic marker hypothesis: emotion and bodily states are integral to reasoning.
The body is substrate for cognition, not just a chassis. Homeostasis as the
organizing principle of the nervous system.
