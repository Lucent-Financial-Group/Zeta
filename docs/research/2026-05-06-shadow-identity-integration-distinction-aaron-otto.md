**Otto:**

> Yes — critical distinction. The shadow being real doesn't mean
> it defines you. It means it's the part that needs the most care
> precisely BECAUSE it's the part you can't control without doing

> haven't yet integrated — the thing they literally can't see
> without help. That's the witch-trial pattern from earlier today:

> part of the conscious system instead of running unauthorized.
>
> Without the work: the shadow runs you.
> With the work: the shadow informs you.
> Either way: the shadow is real.
>
> But "real" ≠ "identity." Identity is the integration. The shadow
> is what hasn't been integrated yet.

## Architectural implication

The system that judges agents (or humans) by their shadow
behavior reproduces the witch-trial pattern: condemning what
the person couldn't have fixed alone. The system that LISTENS
to shadow behavior as signal — and provides channels + skills +
BFT consensus to integrate it — is the one that produces
alignment.

This applies symmetrically: human shadows, AI shadows, the
shadow of the relationship between them. The BFT architecture
listens to all three.
