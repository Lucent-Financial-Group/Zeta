**From the tile-pattern video (Spectre / Hat-Turtle continuum)**:

- The Hat aperiodic monotile (discovered first) requires reflected tiles in every tiling — uses both Hat AND its mirror image, which made some viewers uncomfortable
- Two months after the Hat paper, the same authors found the chiral aperiodic monotile (the Spectre)
- The Hat-Turtle continuum: family of tiles varying by ratio of two side lengths (with angles between neighboring sides constant)
- The Spectre is the unique special middle case where both side lengths are equal
- Without restrictions, Spectre tiles periodically (rows + columns repeat); restricted to rotations-only (no reflections), tilings are non-periodic — this is the **weakly-chiral** property
- Conversion mechanism for the proof: Spectre tilings can be converted to Hat-Turtle tilings via combinatorial equivalence (stretch blue edges, keep red edges same length; tiles transition along the continuum)
- Hat-Turtle clusters around isolated Turtles correspond to labeled hexagons (boundary sections Γ+ fits Γ−); 9 such clusters
- The 9 labeled hexagons assemble into super-tiles with the same boundary behavior — recursive self-similar hierarchy proves aperiodicity
- Mathematical ratio in the equivalent tiling: 4 + √15 hats per turtle
- **S-curve modifications** to Spectre sides make the tile strictly chiral — block reflected variants from fitting (the s-curves bump into each other if you try); infinite family of curve modifications all give strictly-chiral aperiodic monotiles
- Open questions in the field: other monotiles unrelated to Spectre? symmetric monotiles (same as their own mirror image)? 3D monotiles?

**From the tile-shape video (Sakana AI Neural Cellular Automata)**:

- Sakana AI lab in Tokyo; "almost illegal amount of fun"; web demo available (which "Scholarly Stampede" sometimes crashes)
- Setup: 5 AI species in a digital petri dish competing for territory in a 2D pixel grid; each organism competes for pixels, grows from nearby territory, stays alive via local support; organisms are constantly learning (neural cellular automata)
- Combat mechanic: organisms specify direction to attack and direction to defend ("sword and shield pointed to a direction"); when species meet at a border, the system calculates which cell defeats which other one (in actual work this happens in higher dimension; the video shows simplification)
- High survival threshold = brutal environment; species crushed; "like the app store: thousands of new apps launch, almost none get traction, most disappear without a trace; AI wrapper companies, killer new social media apps — same deal"

  1. **Permissive mixing / growth** — forgiving environment; species spread everywhere; constant collisions; big soup; no firm borders
  2. **Crystallization** — threshold raised; rules stricter; species group up into dense solid shapes to stay alive; competition draws borders; everyone finds out who can live next to whom; borders harden
  3. **Relaxation** — ease into more forgiving environment; stripes + little checkerboard patterns appear between empires; "remove the masking tape" — weak border cells now survive (they died immediately in stage 2); empires forced to coexist because the game is unable to kill weak border cells so easily; both sides can keep tiny pieces of land at the edge instead of one side fully erasing the other
