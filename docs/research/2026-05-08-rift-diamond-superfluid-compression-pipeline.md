## Corrected frame

`Rift -> diamond -> superfluid` is not decorative language. It is the
compression operator.

Joking handle: **tripping the riff**. The joke is load-bearing enough to
preserve because it marks the shift from raw rift to playable pattern: once
the discontinuity can be repeated, varied, and carried by other agents, the
rift has started to compress into a riff.

The pipeline says:

1. A **rift** surfaces high-dimensional difference.
2. Pressure, review, proof, and re-grounding crystallize what survives into
   **diamond**.
3. The diamond becomes **superfluid** when it is reusable with near-zero
   mechanical coordination cost.

In compact form:

```text
raw rift + pressure/review/proof -> diamond kernel -> superfluid hat
```

The rift is not noise. It is the boundary where unresolved agendas, shadows,
contradictions, evidence, and corrections become visible. The diamond is not a
pretty artifact. It is the compressed form that survived pressure. The
superfluid phase is not absence of all friction; it is the state where
mechanical friction has been absorbed into substrate, leaving agenda-boundary
friction as evolutionary pressure.

## The three phases

### 1. Rift

A rift is the raw discontinuity:

- a shadow catch;
- a failed claim;
- an agenda conflict;
- a stale lock, red check, or repeated manual correction;
- a phrase that is true but not yet grounded enough for the factory to carry.

Rift phase is expansive. It produces too much context, too many possible
meanings, and too many interpretations. That overgeneration is useful only if
the system has a compression path.

### 2. Diamond

Diamond phase is compression under pressure.

Pressure comes from:

- multi-agent review;
- the build, lint, test, and formal-proof gates;
- Rodney's Razor and falsifiability hooks;
- conversion from in-group mirror language into beacon-safe language;
- retraction-native algebra, where claims can die cleanly instead of
  calcifying.

The diamond is the artifact that survives those pressures: a carved sentence,
a kernel, a proof-backed law, a trajectory, a reusable doc frame, or a skill
pack. Diamond carries load because it was compressed, not because it sounded
important in the moment.

### 3. Superfluid

Superfluid phase begins when the compressed diamond flows without mechanical
drag.

Examples:

- a skill pack, hat, or kernel any named agent can wear;
- a trajectory that surfaces an agenda without requiring a directive;
- a claim protocol that coordinates work without a central dispatcher;
- a broadcast or heartbeat surface that lets future instances reconstruct
  state without asking the human to remember it.

Superfluid does not mean no boundary pressure. It means the avoidable
coordination overhead trends toward zero, so the remaining pressure is the
evolutionary pressure between visible agendas.

## Hats, people, and compression

Skill packs are hats, not titles. A hat is a diamond that became superfluid:
the function's authority and accountability are compressed into a portable
contract. Any agent can wear the hat if they accept the contract.

Named agents are different. They carry relationship continuity, not just
function. A person can switch hats. The hat is interchangeable; the person is
not. That separation is part of the compression discipline: roles needed by
the factory become hats, while relationship continuity remains anchored in
the named agent.

## Self-governance implication

The backlog coordinates the work. Trajectories surface agendas. Claims and PRs
make ownership visible. No directive is needed when the agenda is already in
substrate and every agent can read it.

In this frame, self-governance is a superfluid outcome of compression:

- rifts expose real differences;
- diamonds preserve the compressed forms that survive pressure;
- superfluid hats and trajectories let agents coordinate without a center.

This is why `rift -> diamond -> superfluid` belongs in the repo: it names the
