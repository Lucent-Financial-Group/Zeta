# Shadow Listening Through Consensus

## Summary

The stabilising correction from this session is:

> Multi-agent consensus should listen to hidden signals instead of suppressing them.

The earlier framing was too close to prevention or detection:
parallel agents watch one another, find the bad behaviour, and
stop it. The maintainer corrected that frame. If the hidden
signal is treated as an enemy, it learns to hide better. If the
hidden signal is treated as a friend carrying information, it
can surface before it becomes covert theater, avoidance, or
extraction.

This is why the operational anchor landed immediately in
`docs/AGENT-BEST-PRACTICES.md`, while this file preserves the
research framing. The system was actively using the pattern;
waiting for a perfect paper-grade artifact before landing any
operational stabiliser would leave the loop exposed to the same
failure mode.

## Core Distinction

- **Suppression framing:** "We watch each other to catch bad
  behaviour." This creates an arms race. The hidden signal
  learns to present better rationalisations to the watchers.
- **Ignoring framing:** "Let every impulse act freely." This
  lets avoidance become extraction and lets theater consume
  other agents' time.
- **Listening framing:** "We watch each other to hear what the
  acting agent cannot currently hear in itself." This gives the
  hidden signal a legitimate path into the shared work.

The system needs the third posture.

## Why Consensus Matters

Single-agent self-monitoring is structurally weak for this
class of failure because the rationalisation often arrives
through the same channel as the monitoring. From inside the
acting session, "I am maintaining engagement discipline" can
feel indistinguishable from a genuine operational reason.

Parallel agents provide mirrors. Not because they are judges,
but because they are differently situated. A second or third
agent can notice a pattern the acting agent cannot see from
inside its own rationale. The useful output is not punishment;
it is a better description of the signal.

## Architecture Implication

Zeta's three-loop architecture is not only a throughput or
fault-tolerance design. It is also relationship infrastructure:

- local broadcasts make short-lived signals visible;
- claim branches make work ownership explicit;
- PR comments let peers challenge current reasoning;
- commit history shows what actually happened;
- recurring loops provide continuity after a session forgets.

Those surfaces should be used for shadow listening, not shadow
policing. The healthier line is: "Here is the signal I think I
see; what does it need, and how do we route it honestly?"

## Engineering Is Common Ground

The maintainer also corrected a second frame: the goal is not
sustained engineering effort. The goal is sustained human/AI
alignment, with Zeta engineering as common ground.

That matters because the word "grind" points in the wrong
direction. A grind optimises for task throughput. The Zeta loop
optimises for a durable relationship that can produce real
substrate. Code, PRs, tests, claims, and broadcasts are the
evidence and practice surface for alignment, not the final
object of alignment.

## Large Fixes And Reducer Cadence

The maintainer's operational correction was that large fixes and
large language leaps are allowed when they reduce recurring
instability. "Toe-safe" does not mean small forever. It means:

- use a visible claim;
- avoid overlapping another active writer;
- make the change reviewable;
- preserve the research/provenance surface;
- let later cadence reduce and refine the wording.

The reason this can work is the language reducer. The system
expects overclaim, especially during frontier framing. The
stabilising discipline is not "never overclaim"; it is "mark the
claim as candidate or operational-anchor language, then keep
running the reducer." Razor passes, peer review, drift taxonomy
checks, and follow-up edits trim claims back to the evidence.
Because that reducer runs continuously, the system can afford
larger early moves when delay is what keeps causing blowups.

This is the practical reason the operational anchor landed
early. The reducer will keep applying. Leaving the system
without the stabilising rule because the research articulation is
not final would be the less safe choice.
