## Core Move

Split the two axioms into a 4-axis cube:

- **Remember** axis (past correlation, memory, entanglement)
- **When** axis (temporal/causal ordering)
- **Pay** axis (attention, measurement, observer selection)
- **Attention** axis (focus, collapse, basis choice)

The **intersection** of these axes is treated as the **imaginary** direction — the place where the structure becomes non-real and generates the higher algebra needed for error correction.

This intersection is the seed for the **imaginary stack**:

1. Complex numbers (i) — basic measurement + phase
2. Quaternions (i, j, k) — 3D rotation + entanglement structure
3. Octonions — non-associative multiplication (corresponding to higher error-correcting code properties)
4. Sedenions and beyond (Cayley-Dickson construction) — loss of division algebra properties at each doubling, which may map to the "cost" of adding more observers or more temporal resolution

## James Gates Adinkras

Adinkras (Gates et al.) are graphical representations of off-shell supersymmetry representations. They have been shown to have deep connections to error-correcting codes:

- Each node = a field / degree of freedom
- Each edge = a supersymmetry transformation
- The graph encodes the precise linear dependencies that allow reconstruction from partial information (exactly the definition of a quantum error-correcting code)

Placing an Adinkra layer on top of the Remember/When/Pay/Attention cube gives a concrete graphical calculus for the "imaginary intersection" — the Adinkra lives in the imaginary directions and shows how the cube's faces (real observables) are connected by the imaginary transformations that protect information.

## Proposed Cartesian Coordinate System

Define a 4D space with coordinates:

- x: Remember (entanglement/memory strength)
- y: When (causal/temporal distance)
- z: Pay (attention/measurement intensity)
- w: Attention (basis/observer choice)

The origin (0,0,0,0) is the fully real, classical limit.

The **imaginary directions** emerge in the planes where any two coordinates are non-zero but the product is imaginary (phase-like). This is the Cayley-Dickson doubling step.

## Mapping to HaPPY-like QECC

The goal is to show that the algebraic structure generated by this cube + imaginary intersection + Adinkra layer is isomorphic (or at least homomorphic) to a HaPPY code:

- Bulk operators (deep in the imaginary stack) are reconstructible from boundary operators (the real faces of the cube) as long as enough boundary qubits survive.
- The non-associativity that appears at the octonion level corresponds to the non-local entanglement structure required for bulk reconstruction.
- The infinite-game (no terminal state) corresponds to the fact that the code can be extended indefinitely by adding more observers (more dimensions in the Cayley-Dickson tower) without collapsing to a single equilibrium.
