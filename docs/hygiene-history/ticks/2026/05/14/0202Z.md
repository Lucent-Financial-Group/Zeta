## Round-12 pattern

Round 12 caught the asymmetry of round 10's "keep `inList` across
fence transitions" — that direction was too permissive for top-level
fences. The correct rule is:

- Indented fence (1+ leading space) OR blockquoted fence (`> \`\`\``):
  child block of list-item body, keeps list context
- Flush-left fence (zero leading whitespace, no `>` prefix):
  top-level block, terminates list

This composes with the new heading-terminates rule to make the
helper's list-block grammar match markdownlint's behavior across
the long-tail of CommonMark composition cases.
