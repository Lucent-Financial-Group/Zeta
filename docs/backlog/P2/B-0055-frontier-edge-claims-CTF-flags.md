## CTF rules (retractibility-native)

Any flag can be challenged by filing a retractibly-rewrite revision block on its defense-surface file (memory, ADR, BACKLOG row). The revision is ADDITIVE (old claim retracted with -1 weight, new claim asserted with +1, dated revision line preserved). No flag is destroyed — superseded flags remain in the record as failed-CTF-defense, feeding the filter-failure-rate measurable on the alignment-trajectory dashboard. Staking a flag is free; defending it against good-faith challenge is the real work; both are audit-visible.
