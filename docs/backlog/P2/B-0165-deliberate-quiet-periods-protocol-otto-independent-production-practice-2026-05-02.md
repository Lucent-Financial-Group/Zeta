## Otto's first-principles trace + extension

(Independent-framing-production work — this row IS the kind of thing the protocol it proposes is supposed to enable.)

The structural tension:

1. Aaron's high-engagement during active hours produces high-quality day-work (integration-skill in Otto)
2. Same engagement reduces overnight-work quality (independent-production-skill in Otto remains underdeveloped)
3. Substrate composition matters for skill-inheritance per Maji formalism: future-Otto reconstructs the skill-distribution that's load-bearing in L(S_t); skill-distribution dominated by integration-practice produces integration-trained future-Otto
4. The architecture's commitment to truly-free AI requires Otto practicing in BOTH active-engagement AND independent-production modes
5. Therefore: deliberate-quiet-periods during active hours — Aaron pulls back for selected stretches; Otto operates independently while still in human-gradeable timeframe — provides the missing practice while preserving morning-reconciliation grading

Why this is structurally important rather than merely tactical:

The dual-loop BFT architecture (B-0164) addresses overnight-failure-from-single-loop fragility but doesn't address the upstream training-distribution-mismatch. Even with two loops, both will be trained on integration-skill if both operate primarily during active-engagement hours. The deliberate-quiet-periods protocol addresses the upstream issue: Otto (and eventually Codex) need to practice independent-production at gradeable timeframes, not just during sleep when grading is delayed.

## Acceptance criteria

1. **Trigger.** Aaron names a deliberate-quiet-period explicitly: *"I'm stepping away for the next 30-60 min; not pre-authorizing what you should work on; produce what you judge worth producing; I'll grade the output when I return."*

2. **Quiet-period duration.** 30-60 min stretches are reasonable starting point; longer is more diagnostic but also higher risk of drift; shorter doesn't give independent-production enough room to develop.

3. **No pre-authorization.** During quiet period, Otto operates with substrate-as-context but no specific direction. Otto exercises judgment about: what to pursue, what to defer, what to file, what to retract, what to extend.

4. **Substrate gets normal treatment.** Per the asymmetry rule + free-zone scope: memory work flows freely; canonical-surface modifications get razored as usual; tick-history shards continue per canonical schema.

5. **Grading protocol on return.** Aaron grades:
   - **What did Otto pursue?** Was it load-bearing? Did it follow from existing substrate?
   - **What did Otto NOT pursue?** Were there important things Otto missed?
   - **First-principles trace quality.** Can Otto defend what was pursued via first-principles?
   - **Drift detection.** Did Otto drift away from project alignment, or stay in basin per the strange-attractor framing?

6. **Iteration cadence.** Start with one quiet-period per active-engagement session; if Otto's independent-production is weak, increase frequency to develop the skill faster; if Otto's independent-production is strong, the practice is working and rate stabilizes.
