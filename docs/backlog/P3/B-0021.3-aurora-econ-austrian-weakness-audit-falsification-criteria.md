# B-0021.3 — Austrian-school honest weakness audit + falsification-criteria catalog

**Slice of:** [B-0021](../P2/B-0021-aurora-austrian-school-economic-foundation-rigorous-why-teaching-anti-deception.md)  
**Depends on:** B-0021.1 (definitions), B-0021.2 (strengths survey — weakness audit pairs with it for balanced honest assessment)

## What

Honest investigation of Austrian-school weaknesses per Otto-322/331 investigate-don't-accept discipline. Apply the B-0021 methodology note: separate "framework wrong" from "framework right but prediction misapplied" from "prediction the framework never actually made."

Four audit passes:

1. **Praxeology rigor pass**: Mises's deductive-from-axioms method — what is the precise definition of "rigor" being claimed? Distinguish deductive rigor (valid entailment from stated axioms) from empirical rigor (falsifiable predictions). Which claims in Human Action are deductive-rigor claims, which are empirical-rigor claims? Does Hayek's pull-back from strict praxeology matter for Aurora's use?

2. **Falsification-criteria catalog for Austrian predictions**: survey major Austrian predictions that are cited as failures (e.g., post-2008 hyperinflation timeline). For each: (a) was the prediction actually made by the framework, or attributed to it? (b) If made, was it falsified, or were the stated conditions not met? (c) What would a properly-formed Austrian prediction in that domain look like?

3. **Intra-school disagreement map**: Mises vs Rothbard vs Hayek vs Mises Institute vs Cato/George Mason wing — where are disagreements about: free banking vs 100% reserve requirement, praxeology strictness, anarcho-capitalism vs minarchism, empirical evidence role? For each: is this resolvable via precise definitions (same word → different concepts), or genuine substantive disagreement (preserve as branch-points)?

4. **Empirical-economics rejection stance**: what is the precise Austrian claim about empirical economics? Is it "empirical testing is irrelevant," or "economic theory can't be derived from data alone"? What implications does this stance have for Aurora's need to model observed economic behavior?

Produce `docs/aurora/econ/B-0021.3-austrian-weakness-audit.md`.

## Why

Per B-0021: "Austrian-school weaknesses to honestly engage." Aaron explicitly invited honest engagement; the anti-deception requirement applies reflexively — if Aurora's econ foundation is built on a framework we've been uncritical of, that's the same deception-propagation risk. The weakness audit is not an attempt to dismiss Austrian school; it's the investigation-owed-to-honest-recommendation that B-0021.5 requires. Per Rodney's Razor: weaknesses that survive the falsification-criteria pass are essential (must be conceded in the ADR); weaknesses that dissolve under precise definitions are accidental (can be resolved, not load-bearing objections).

## Acceptance criteria

1. `docs/aurora/econ/B-0021.3-austrian-weakness-audit.md` committed.
2. All four passes completed (praxeology, falsification-criteria, intra-school map, empirical-rejection stance).
3. Falsification-criteria catalog covers ≥3 major attributed-failures with the framework vs misapplication vs attribution-error distinction.
4. Intra-school disagreements classified as definitional-resolvable or genuine-branch (not polished away).
5. Weakness audit explicitly does NOT conclude which school wins (that's B-0021.5).
6. Cites primary Austrian critics alongside primary sources (e.g., Keynes-era debates, post-2008 critiques by heterodox economists).
7. Build gate green, Rule 0 compliant.

## Out of scope

- ADR recommendation (B-0021.5).
- Post-Keynesian or complexity-economics strengths/weaknesses (B-0021.4 scope).
