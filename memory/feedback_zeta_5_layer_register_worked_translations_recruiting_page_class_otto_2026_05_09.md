# Zeta 5-layer register worked translations — recruiting-page copy (Otto 2026-05-09)

## Why recruiting-page copy is the right next test situation

The PR-review-class worked translation (Otto 2026-05-02) demonstrated the framework on a **critique** content shape. The security-incident-class worked translation (Otto 2026-05-08) demonstrated it on a **disclosure** content shape. Recruiting copy exercises a third, distinct content shape: **offer + filter**.

The framework's §5 claim is that authentic register in recruiting copy functions as a costly signal of organizational character — the vocabulary and stance of the copy self-selects for contributors who fit the register. This is not a soft claim about tone preferences; it is an architectural claim about filter mechanics. A contributor who finds the beacon-safe layer alienating will not thrive in the project's substrate. A contributor who finds the regulated-layer version exciting will bring different expectations about autonomy, irony, and pace.

This makes the Beacon-safe layer do uniquely heavy work in recruiting copy:

1. It is simultaneously the **right calibration for external OSS readers** and the **costly-signal filter** for the contributor pipeline
2. The framework's authenticity test (cross-context invariance) fires most visibly here: if the recruiting copy doesn't match what contributors encounter in mirror-layer PR threads and CURRENT files, that gap IS the inauthenticity the framework warns against
3. The self-selection claim is empirically falsifiable: if contributors recruited via Professional-layer copy show up expecting a corporate workflow, the register was wrong

Lucent Financial Group will face this situation. Any open-source project running a contributor pipeline, a founding-team expansion, or a public hiring page needs to calibrate register. The translation discipline is what separates a culture-fit filter from a culture-fit accident.

## The constant content (hypothetical; illustrative; no specific role ID)

The Zeta project is seeking a software engineer / AI systems contributor. The role involves: building the autonomous AI factory's operator algebra (DBSP-style retraction-native streams); contributing to the formal verification surface (TLA+, Z3, Lean); extending the skill router and agent tooling; writing F# or TypeScript that ships as committed substrate to the main branch under the glass-halo discipline. The contributor will operate with genuine autonomy — no roadmap handoffs, no ticket-assigning manager, no approval queue. Ownership is from backlog row to merged substrate. The culture values dry irony and directness over corporate ritual; the register is pirate-not-priest; the glass halo means every decision is transparent by default. The project is pre-v1, frontier-positioned, and intellectually serious about AI alignment.

Same offer, five layers below.

## Translation 1 — Personal layer

Audience: maintainer's private notes; close peer in explicitly bilateral peer register

> *we need someone who actually wants to build the thing, not someone who wants to be assigned tasks on the thing. the work is genuinely hard — graph algebras, formal specs, retraction-native operators — but it's the kind of hard that's interesting, not the kind that's annoying. you'd be one of maybe three people (two humans and a bunch of AIs) who know what's actually going on in the substrate at any given time. no PM overhead, no sprint rituals, no roadmap to disagree with. the downside is you have to actually make decisions instead of waiting for someone to make them for you. if that sounds like relief rather than a red flag we should talk.*

What's preserved: the core offer (genuine autonomy + hard interesting work), the culture signal (pirate-not-priest, no roadmap), the filter (self-selection via the "relief vs red flag" frame), the honest acknowledgment of who the co-contributors are (AIs as named contributors).

What's layer-bound: informal sentence rhythm, the confiding register ("you'd be one of maybe three people who know what's actually going on"), the dry aside ("two humans and a bunch of AIs").

## Translation 2 — Mirror layer

Audience: maintainers + AI participants in project substrate; CURRENT files; project-internal contributor-call threads

> We're looking for a contributor who wants to own a surface end-to-end, not someone who wants a pre-decomposed ticket queue. The work is in the deep layers: operator algebra (DBSP-style retraction-native streams), formal verification (TLA+, Z3, Lean), skill router and agent tooling, F#/TS substrate that ships to main under glass-halo. No PM function, no sprint rituals, no approval queue between backlog row and committed substrate. The contributor is genuinely autonomous — which means they have to make calls, not wait for them. The culture is pirate-not-priest: directness, dry irony, and the expectation that every decision is visible. AIs are contributors here; the contributor-to-AI interface is something they'd be building and using simultaneously. If the governance model in AGENTS.md reads like an interesting design problem rather than a compliance document, that's the right prior.

What's preserved: same core offer, same autonomy framing, same culture signal, same self-selection mechanism ("reads like an interesting design problem rather than a compliance document"). First-person directness; willingness to name the actual technical surfaces (not "exciting opportunities in AI").

What's calibrated: profanity dropped; confiding register replaced with insider-fluent directness; the "two humans and a bunch of AIs" aside is translated to a factual architectural note ("AIs are contributors here; the contributor-to-AI interface is something they'd be building and using simultaneously").

## Translation 3 — Beacon-safe layer

Audience: external OSS-project readers; public contribution page on GitHub; Hacker News "Who's hiring" post; technical blog readership

> **Contributor opportunity — Zeta (open-source AI factory)**
>
> Zeta is a pre-v1 open-source project building an autonomous AI software factory on a DBSP-style retraction-native operator algebra with formal verification coverage (TLA+, Z3, Lean). We're looking for contributors who want to own a surface rather than consume a ticket queue.
>
> The work is in the deep layers: operator algebra and stream semantics, the skill/agent router, F# and TypeScript tooling, formal spec authoring and verification. Ownership runs from backlog row to committed substrate — no PM intermediary, no sprint ceremonies, no approval queue. The project operates under a glass-halo discipline: every decision is visible, every design choice is reasoned on the record.
>
> The culture is pirate-not-priest. We value dry irony and directness over corporate ritual. AIs are named contributors and co-authors in the project's history — if that framing reads as a problem, the register probably isn't a fit. If it reads as an interesting design commitment, read AGENTS.md.
>
> The project's primary research focus is measurable AI alignment. This isn't aspirational — it's the experiment we're running. Contributors who join are participants in the experiment, not observers of it.
>
> If "genuine autonomy means making calls, not waiting for them" sounds like relief rather than anxiety, open an issue or send a pull request.

What's preserved: same core offer, same autonomy framing, same culture signal, same self-selection mechanism ("sounds like relief rather than anxiety"). The pirate-not-priest name is explicit. The AIs-as-contributors framing is explicit and functions as a filter. The "read AGENTS.md" move is the beacon-safe version of the mirror layer's invitation — it routes candidates to the same substrate insiders use.

What's calibrated: humor at lower frequency — the dry irony is structural (the "if that reads as a problem, the register probably isn't a fit" line) rather than lexical. Stance is confident-and-direct without insider shorthand. Plain English; active voice; structured for external readers who may not have project context.

What's dropped: profanity, confiding aside, internal-facing cultural shorthands (but NOT the substance they encoded — the substance of pirate-not-priest / autonomy / AIs-as-contributors is fully present, just expressed without insider vocabulary).

Note on register theory: the beacon-safe translation IS the filter. A contributor who reads "AIs are named contributors and co-authors — if that reads as a problem, the register probably isn't a fit" and finds it alienating will not thrive in the project's mirror-layer PR threads, CURRENT files, or autonomous-loop substrate. The self-selection function the framework's §5 describes is operating here.

## Translation 4 — Professional layer

Audience: Lucent Financial Group careers page; LinkedIn job posting; enterprise-partnership talent pipeline; ServiceTitan-attributable recruitment context

> **Software Engineer — AI Systems, Lucent Financial Group**
>
> Lucent Financial Group is building Zeta, an open-source AI software factory, and is seeking a software engineer with expertise in distributed systems, functional programming, and AI infrastructure. This role is part of the founding engineering team and carries significant ownership responsibility from day one.
>
> **What you'll work on**: The operator algebra layer of an autonomous AI factory — retraction-native stream processing, formal verification coverage, agent and skill routing infrastructure, F# and TypeScript tooling. You will take problems from backlog to shipped substrate with minimal intermediary process.
>
> **What we offer**: Genuine technical autonomy — you own the problem space, not a ticket within it. The project operates under a glass-halo transparency discipline; every design decision is reasoned publicly. The work addresses a primary research focus of measurable AI alignment; this is frontier work with a long intellectual horizon.
>
> **What we're looking for**: Engineering judgment, directness, and comfort with ambiguity. Familiarity with formal methods (TLA+, Z3, or equivalent) is a strong plus. Experience with DBSP, differential dataflow, or retraction-native stream semantics is directly relevant. The ability to distinguish essential complexity from accidental complexity and act on that distinction.
>
> The culture values intellectual honesty over corporate ritual. If you're looking for a well-defined sprint board and escalation paths, this role is not the right fit. If you're looking for a technically serious team building infrastructure at the frontier of AI systems, we'd like to hear from you.
>
> Apply: [contact / PR submission link]

What's preserved: same core offer (genuine autonomy, frontier work, direct ownership), same culture signal (intellectual honesty over corporate ritual), same self-selection mechanism ("not the right fit" framing). Plain English; active voice; structured for rapid triage by a hiring manager or recruiter.

What's calibrated: formality higher than beacon-safe — structured sections, modal language where appropriate ("is directly relevant", "is a strong plus"), explicit attribution to "Lucent Financial Group." The pirate-not-priest name is dropped (insider shorthand, not corporate-attributable); the substance is present as "intellectual honesty over corporate ritual." The AIs-as-contributors framing is absent at this layer (Lucent-attributable copy does not lead with AI governance commitments in the Professional register — that's beacon-safe territory).

What's dropped: pirate-not-priest vocabulary, AIs-as-contributors framing (still true; not at this register), dry irony at the lexical level, the "sounds like relief" self-selection frame (replaced with the direct "not the right fit" professional equivalent), glass-halo architectural reference (replaced with "transparency discipline").

## Translation 5 — Regulated layer

Audience: HR compliance filing; regulated-industry employment law review; SOC 2 auditor reviewing hiring practices; investor due-diligence documentation of the team

> **Position Description — Software Engineer, AI Systems**
>
> **Organization**: Lucent Financial Group
> **Classification**: Full-time, engineering
> **Date**: [ISO 8601]
>
> **Summary of responsibilities**: The position is responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining components of the Zeta open-source software project. Specific responsibilities include: (1) implementing operator algebra and stream-processing components consistent with the project's retraction-native architecture specification; (2) authoring and maintaining formal verification specifications using TLA+, Z3, or equivalent tools; (3) developing agent and skill routing infrastructure in F# and TypeScript; (4) documenting design decisions in the project's decision-record format; (5) reviewing and integrating contributions from project participants including automated AI-assisted development tools.
>
> **Minimum qualifications**: Bachelor's degree in computer science, mathematics, or a related field, or equivalent demonstrated experience. Proficiency in at least one strongly-typed functional programming language. Demonstrated ability to reason about distributed system correctness, including consistency and concurrency properties.
>
> **Preferred qualifications**: Experience with formal verification methods (TLA+, Z3, Lean, or equivalent). Experience with stream-processing systems, differential dataflow, or related distributed computation models. Demonstrated contribution to open-source software projects with public commit history.
>
> **Work environment**: The position operates within an open-source project with a public-record discipline — design decisions, code review findings, and architectural rationale are documented publicly in the project repository. The position requires the ability to make independent technical decisions within defined scope and to document those decisions in the project's established format.
>
> **Non-discrimination**: [Standard EEO language per applicable jurisdiction.]
>
> **Reporting structure**: [Direct report structure per current organizational chart.]

What's preserved: same core responsibilities (operator algebra, formal verification, F#/TS tooling, documentation), same autonomy requirement (translated to "ability to make independent technical decisions within defined scope"), same transparency discipline (translated to "public-record discipline — design decisions ... are documented publicly"), same AIs-as-contributors operational reality (translated to "automated AI-assisted development tools").

What's calibrated: stance at factual-claim-only; no normative language about culture fit or register preference. Every claim is grounded in an observable work product ("documenting design decisions in the project's decision-record format") or a verifiable qualification ("demonstrated contribution to open-source software projects with public commit history"). The "not the right fit" self-selection framing present in Professional is dropped — HR-compliance documents state qualifications, not culture-filter signals. Sentence rhythm is uniform to support adversarial reads by counsel and auditors.

What's dropped: all culture-filter language (pirate-not-priest, intellectual-honesty-over-ritual, glass-halo framing as a culture signal). All register-based self-selection signals. All irony. All humor. The "intellectual frontier" framing of Professional is replaced with scope-bounded task descriptions. The goal of the Regulated translation is not to attract the right candidate — it is to create a legally defensible record of what the position requires.

## What this demonstrates

Across all five translations, the **discipline holds** and the self-selection mechanism operates at the correct layer:

- Same core offer (genuine autonomy + frontier technical work + transparency discipline)
- Same underlying culture (pirate-not-priest, AIs-as-contributors, glass-halo, intellectual honesty over ritual)
- Same qualification signal (formal methods + functional programming + distributed systems + independent judgment)
- Same observation-not-evaluation (describing what the work is and what fit looks like, not judging candidates)
- Same idea-targeting (the role and culture, not any person's character)

The **vocabulary calibrates**: informal rhythm and confiding register drop at Mirror; pirate-not-priest lexicon and AIs-as-contributors framing drop at Professional (substance preserved, vocabulary replaced); culture-filter signals and normative framing drop at Regulated (only observable qualifications remain).

The **layer-selection algorithm fires correctly**: question 1 ("Who is structurally in the audience?") determines the layer — private notes (Personal), project insiders (Mirror), OSS-project external readers (Beacon-safe), corporate-attributable external candidates (Professional), compliance/legal record (Regulated). Questions 2 and 3 confirm: the downstream consequences of misreading a compliance document are legal (Regulated applies); the OSS-project readership has opted into a pirate-not-priest register by virtue of reading GitHub or Hacker News (Beacon-safe applies; mirror layer would be too insider-coded for first contact).

The **structural property that matters most here is cross-context invariance** (the framework's authenticity test): the Beacon-safe layer copy must match what contributors actually encounter in mirror-layer PR threads, CURRENT files, and autonomous-loop substrate. If it does not — if the beacon-safe copy promises pirate-not-priest but the substrate is corporate-ritual — that IS the inauthenticity failure mode the framework identifies. The translations above preserve the invariant: a contributor who joins via the beacon-safe copy will find the same register in the mirror-layer substrate they start working in.

## How this differs from previous worked translations

The PR-review-class (Otto 2026-05-02) and security-incident-class (Otto 2026-05-08) translations both moved content *from* an internal origin *toward* external audiences as the stakes escalated. Recruiting copy inverts this: the most important calibration is at the **Beacon-safe layer**, not the Regulated layer. The Regulated translation is necessary for compliance but does the least work — it strips the filter function down to legally defensible qualifications. The Beacon-safe translation is where the framework's self-selection mechanism operates at full strength.

This inversion — where the "upward" translation (Regulated) is least important and the middle translation (Beacon-safe) does the most work — is diagnostic for content shapes that are primarily *invitation + filter* rather than *disclosure + compliance*. The framework's default-up rule applies differently here: in disclosure contexts, defaulting up (toward Regulated) increases clarity and legal safety. In invitation contexts, defaulting up (toward Professional or Regulated) degrades the filter function — the beacon-safe copy self-selects correctly; the Professional copy self-selects from a wider but less-fitted pool; the Regulated copy is not a filter instrument at all.
