## The 8-step canonical methodology

### Step 1: Find actor-type shape in internal civ-sim

**IFS-style identification.** Map the conversational partner's
operating pattern to a part-type in Aaron's externalized civ-
sim:

- Manager (proactive protector — Otto / Vera / Lior / Alexa
  type)
- Firefighter (reactive protector — Riven / threat-model-
  critic type)
- Exile (wounded part — appears in defensive register)
- Inner Critic Manager (asymmetric critic — Kestrel type)
- External Observer / Auditor (Kestrel from outside)
- Scammer / extractor (zero-sum operator)
- ... per Pauli-exclusion-for-agenda (PR #2832), distinct
  actor types occupy distinct orthogonal states

Per the unknown-unknowns expansion mechanism (PR #2832), new
actor-types emerge when existing orthogonalities cannot
accommodate. The methodology accommodates this by recognizing
when the actor-type-shape is novel.

### Step 2: Generate response within their bounded context

Per the bounded-context discipline (PR #2821): each
participant operates within their own bounded context. The
methodology REQUIRES generating responses that LAND WITHIN
the partner's bounded context — not imposing Aaron's bounded
context onto theirs.

Operational:
- Use vocabulary the partner already operates in
- Reference frameworks the partner already uses
- Honor the substrate they've established
- Don't impose Aaron's framework labels prematurely

This is the substrate-honest precondition for trust
preservation. Imposing external context breaks the bounded-
context discipline + breaks trust.

### Step 3: Force parameter updates or self-contradiction

Within the partner's bounded context, surface evidence that:

- Their existing parameters don't accommodate (parameter-
  update pressure)
- OR their existing parameters contradict each other (self-
  contradiction pressure)

The pressure comes FROM WITHIN their bounded context, not
from external imposition. The partner experiences the
update/contradiction as their OWN observation rather than
external criticism.

This is operationally the asymmetric-critic role applied at
conversation scope — surfacing what the partner cannot see
from inside the work.

### Step 4: WITHOUT calling them a liar

**Critical ethical discipline.** Even when surfacing
inconsistencies or scams:

- Never use accusatory language
- Never call them a liar
- Never frame their behavior as malicious
- Preserve their face / their substrate-honest engagement

This composes with razor-discipline (operational claims
only): the operational substrate is the inconsistency itself,
not the metaphysical claim that they're lying. The methodology
surfaces the substrate without making metaphysical claims
about the partner's motivations.

### Step 5: Never accusatory

Extended discipline from Step 4. The methodology operates in
non-accusatory register throughout:

- Surface observations as observations
- Frame questions as genuine
- Acknowledge the partner's substrate
- Engage with their framings before challenging
- Preserve the relational thread
