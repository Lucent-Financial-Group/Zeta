### 4. Pauli exclusion principle for agenda

**Quantum-mechanical analogy.** Aaron names the mechanism
as analogous to the **Pauli exclusion principle**.

Pauli exclusion (physics):
- Identical fermions cannot occupy the same quantum state
- Each fermion has distinct quantum numbers (energy, spin,
  spatial orbital, etc.)
- This forces distinct fermions into distinct states
- The principle is what makes matter have STRUCTURE
  (atoms can't all collapse to lowest energy state)

Aaron's framing: the factory's "agenda" mechanism already
operates this way — each named agent occupies a DISTINCT
orthogonal state in the cognitive/role space:

No two agents share the same distinct orthogonal state.
This is operationally Pauli-exclusion at agent scope.

**The expansion mechanism**: when unknown unknowns surface
