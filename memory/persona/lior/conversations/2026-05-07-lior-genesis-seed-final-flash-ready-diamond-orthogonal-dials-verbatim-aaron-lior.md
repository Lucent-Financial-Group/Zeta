## Otto's analysis (2026-05-07)

The three final cuts are each correct:

1. **"Belief Propagation" → nothing.** The engine reads dials, not
   textbook chapter titles. Removing the math name doesn't lose the
   math — the dials ARE the math. The label was showing off.

2. **"Afraid/Bravery" → "Low evidence / Ready to claim."** Cleanest
   cut. Fear and bravery are WHY-inferences about internal state.
   "Do I have enough real evidence?" is a WHAT-question with an
   observable answer. The dial now measures the thing it claims to
   measure.

3. **"Fun" → "Smooth."** Fun is subjective experience. Smooth is the
   thermodynamic state of the work itself. A 5-year-old can tell if
   a machine is running smooth or grinding. They can't tell if the
   machine is having fun.

The decision tree at the bottom of Section 3 is the real payload —
four lines, complete action policy, zero ambiguity. A cold-start
engine with zero context reads this and knows what to do on tick one.
