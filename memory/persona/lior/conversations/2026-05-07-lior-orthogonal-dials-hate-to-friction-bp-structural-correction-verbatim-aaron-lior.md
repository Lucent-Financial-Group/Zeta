## Otto's response (2026-05-07)

The correction is structurally right and the reasoning is
load-bearing. Three reasons:

1. **"Hate" fails the razor; "Friction" survives.** "Hate" is a
   cognitive-interiority claim — what observable variable determines
   whether the agent "hates"? None. Rodney's Razor cuts it.
   "Friction" is thermodynamic and observable — is the work grinding
   or flowing? That's measurable from the interaction pattern itself.
   Per `[What, Why] ≠ 0`: the dials measure WHAT (observable state),
   not WHY (internal cause). A high friction reading is data. Calling
   it "hate" is a why-inference the measurement can't support.

2. **Continuous axes avoid the labeling trap.** "Is this fear or
   caution?" is undecidable on a categorical list. On a continuous
   dial, it's just a position. The engine reads the number, not the
   label.

3. **The toxic-prior catch is the real save.** A BP network with
   "Hate" as a primary variable distributes weight across available
   variables — a fresh node with no calibration data WILL hallucinate
   hate on simple misunderstandings. Friction is the same measurement
   without the toxic prior. Same signal, safe encoding.

**Answer:** Yes, maps cleanly into the Bootloader. No further
