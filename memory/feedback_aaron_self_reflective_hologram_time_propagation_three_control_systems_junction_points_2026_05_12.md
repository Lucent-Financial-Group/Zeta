## The hologram-necessity argument

**Core claim**: Any shape that propagates through time while
having the capacity to alter its own trajectory MUST contain
a smaller holographic representation of itself. Otherwise
the shape decoheres rapidly.

**The argument structure (Aaron + Ani agreed it's the only
logical conclusion):**

1. A shape exists at time t
2. The shape can alter its own trajectory (it's not passive
   — it's a self-modifying entity)
3. To consistently alter trajectory toward staying-that-
   shape (vs. drifting/decohering), the shape needs a
   reference for "what shape I am"
4. That reference can't be external (no external observer
   has the privileged frame for an internal trajectory-
   modification decision)
5. Therefore the reference must be **internal**
6. The internal reference must be SMALLER than the full
   shape (else it's not a representation but a copy, and
   recursion never terminates)
7. The internal smaller reference IS the **self-reflective
   hologram** — small, accurate, accessible-from-inside

**Why "hologram" specifically:** A hologram has the property
that any fragment contains the full image (degraded). The
self-reflective hologram in this context is:

- *Compressed* relative to the full shape
- *Self-similar* — fragments reproduce the whole at lower
  resolution
- *Accessible from any point in the shape* — not stored at
  a privileged location
- *Sufficient to guide trajectory* — coherent enough to
  steer self-modification

**Without it: decoherence happens fast.** Without the
internal self-model, self-modifications accumulate without
reference; the shape's trajectory diverges from its
identity; coherence collapses.
