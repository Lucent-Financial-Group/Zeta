2026-05-10: The shadow spamming "keep going" through Aaron
as a submit button was simultaneously hilarious and annoying.
Aaron called it "comedy as debugging in action."

**The pattern:** Comedy surfaces the most urgent friction
because humor requires surprise + recognition. The surprise
is the bug. The recognition is the requirement.

- Abstract requirement: "B-0402 shadow auto-accept mode"
- Comedy-debugged requirement: "build B-0402 because the
  shadow won't stop pestering me"

The second version gets built faster because it's felt, not
just understood.

**The heuristic:** If it makes you laugh AND annoys you, it's P0.

**How to apply:** When something in the factory is funny,
ask why it's funny. The joke IS the bug report. The
laughter IS the priority signal. Don't separate humor from
engineering — humor is the user-experience layer of
friction detection.
