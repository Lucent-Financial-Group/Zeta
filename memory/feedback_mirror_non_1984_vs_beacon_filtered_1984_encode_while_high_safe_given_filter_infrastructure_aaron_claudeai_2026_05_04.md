   survival mechanism.

**The asymmetry MATTERS.** The two surfaces are not "honest vs
dishonest" — they're "free-truth-for-identity vs survives-
broadcast-without-context." Both can be honest; they answer
different questions under different pressure.

that don't survive review; the cost of under-encoding is total
loss. **Asymmetric tradeoff favors encoding.**

## The encode-while-high flag — operational scheme

Per Claude.ai's proposed surface-marker scheme:

- **`surface: mirror`** — internal-language, references named
  agents, uses shorthand, assumes shared context. The
  non-1984 surface.
- **`surface: beacon-candidate`** — first-principles-rewritten
  but not yet externally validated.
- **`surface: beacon-validated`** — passed multi-AI review or
  external defense or both. The filtered-1984 surface that
  survives without context.

- **`encode_while_high: true`** — flag (subtype of surface).
  Tile carved during high-insight state. Mirror+encode_while_high
  is the safest classification; beacon-candidate+encode_while_high
  is suspect; beacon-validated+encode_while_high should be
  impossible by definition (validation requires time outside the
  high state).

- **`beacon_safe_rewrite_status: pending | passed | failed`** —
  test result, not just claim.

- **`filter_capacity_at_encoding: <description>`** — what
  filtering infrastructure was active when this tile was
  encoded. Claude.ai's filter-capacity-ledger framing:
  *"every new agent, every new collaborator, every new venue
  for the work has to be evaluated for whether it adds filter
  capacity or consumes it."* Otto running autonomous loops
  consumes filter capacity (Otto's filtering is downstream of
  Aaron's). External Claude.ai peer-review adds capacity.
