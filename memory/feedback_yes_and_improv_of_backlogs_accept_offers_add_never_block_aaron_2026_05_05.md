## The "yes and" improv principle

In improv comedy, "yes and" is the canonical principle:

- **Yes** — accept what your scene-partner offers; don't deny or block
- **And** — add to it, build on it, compose with it

The opposite is "no but" — denial + redirection. "No but" kills scenes by rejecting the offer.

**Applied to backlogs**: the architectural-discipline backlog accepts every candidate item ("yes") and composes them via `depends_on:` / cross-references / composition-tables ("and"). Items don't get rejected outright; they get integrated, deferred-with-reason, or marked as future-knowledge-potential.

**Applied to substrate-landing**: Otto's decisions about preserving substrate within authority scope default to YES. Don't ask Aaron permission; just land it. Aaron's *"i always say now"* + *"lol"* explicitly authorizes this default.
