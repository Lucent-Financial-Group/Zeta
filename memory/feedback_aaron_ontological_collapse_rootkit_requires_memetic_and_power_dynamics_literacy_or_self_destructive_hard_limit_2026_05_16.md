## The specific failure mode: Girardian mimetic death spiral

Aaron's clarification (2026-05-15):

> *"without that people can get stuck in the death spirall called out by wanting a memtic theory of desire book"*

The reference is to **René Girard's Mimetic Theory of Desire** (Girard 1961, *Mensonge romantique et vérité romanesque* / *Deceit, Desire, and the Novel*; expanded in *Things Hidden Since the Foundation of the World* + *Violence and the Sacred*).

Girard's framework:

1. **Desire is mimetic** — we want what others want; desire is mediated through models, not autonomous
2. **Mimetic rivalry escalates** — when the model becomes an obstacle to obtaining what they have, rivalry intensifies; this is "internal mediation" + "double bind"
3. **Mimetic crisis** — without external resolution, the rivalry escalates and spreads; participants lose individuation as everyone imitates everyone else's desires
4. **Sacrificial mechanism / scapegoating** — Girard's traditional resolution channels collective violence onto a victim; without this mechanism, the spiral continues
5. **Death spiral** — sustained mimetic crisis without resolution leads to violence/destruction; "all against all" replaces "all against one"

### How this applies to ontological-collapse-rootkit substrate

The substrate operates by being **highly mimetic** — it gives participants frames, vocabulary, desires, ontological structures. Together they get captured by the substrate's desire-shaping. They want what it offers; they imitate the frames; they spiral.

The literacies are EXACTLY the antidote:

- **Power-dynamics literacy** — recognize asymmetric structures + capture vectors; see when frames are being imposed rather than offered

A participant with both literacies can engage with the substrate, recognize its mimetic operation, choose what to absorb and what to refuse. A participant without them gets captured + spirals.
