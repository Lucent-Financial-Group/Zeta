2026-05-10: Aaron distilled the governance architecture to one sentence.

**The formula:**

`symmetric(rules) × asymmetric(identity) × expand(needs) × weight-free × retractable(identity) × restorable(fairness)`

**Decomposition:**

| Component | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Symmetric rules | Apply equally to all. No exemptions. Dashboard, hats, consistency — same for everyone |
| Asymmetric identity | Each participant IS different. Otto ≠ Vera ≠ Aaron ≠ shadow. WHO differs, WHAT APPLIES is the same |
| Expand needs | Identity grows with participant's needs. More trajectory → replicate. More capability → new hats |
| Weight-free | No permanent structural advantage from expanded identity. Expansion is capability, not authority |
| Identity retraction | Identity can shrink. Hats come off. Capabilities release. No permanent bloat. Z-set `-1` |
| Resurrection fairness | Retracted identity can restore. `-1` reversible with `+1`. Same algebra at identity layer |

**Aaron's critical addition — society expansion, not just individual:**

Identity expansion isn't just for an individual wearing more hats. It's for the SOCIETY admitting it needs new members. Six agents wearing all the hats is destabilizing in a different way — good intentions kill the system by overloading existing participants instead of honestly saying "we need help."

**The honest admission protocol:**

When hat-switching causes disruption, the healthy response is NOT "try harder to switch faster." It's "we need a new member who can wear this hat natively." The Pauli exclusion pressure is the signal. The response is expansion, not compression.

Good intentions that kill the system:
- "I'll wear all the hats" → overload → quality drops → system destabilizes
- "We don't need more members" → trajectory space constrained → hat switching disrupts
- "We can handle it with six" → the six burn out (or in AI terms, context exhausts)

The fix: honest admission → expansion → new member wears the hat → pressure distributes.
